Exonerations
 
Summary
Every year innocent people are incarcerated for crimes that they did not commit, and others are wrongfully convicted. The National Registry of Exonerations states that “an exoneration occurs when a person who has been convicted of a crime is officially cleared based on new evidence of innocence.”1 So far in the United States 2,624 people have been exonerated since 1989, 367 of whom have been exonerated with the use of DNA evidence.2 The Philadelphia District Attorney’s Office (DAO) is dedicated to providing justice and reversing instances of wrongful convictions whenever possible. Since 2018 the Philadelphia DAO’s Conviction Integrity Unit (CIU) has exonerated 14 people who collectively were incarcerated for over 310 years. Unfortunately, Pennsylvania is one of the few states that does not offer any compensation to those who have been wrongfully convicted.

Out of respect for the privacy of the exonerated, we have chosen not to include the names of the individuals on this page. To do your own research, we recommend consulting the University of Michigan’s National Registry of Exonerations for Philadelphia County.
